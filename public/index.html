Pregnancy and herbs

Herbs to avoid or restrict during pregnancy

Generally safe

These are considered generally safe at normal doses. Note that most of the herbs below may induce contractions, which can be painful. If normal consumption isn't causing contractions, usage should be relatively fine.

May induce contractions

May induce contractions

Aloe vera

Also known as: Sábila, zábila, Aloe, babosa
Topical gel use is considered safe Ingestion (leaf or tea) may induce contractions or cause miscarriage

Risky (see notes)

The following are generally safe, but have uses that are risky. Please see the notes on each ingredient, to find out which applications are considered unsafe.

Chasteberry

Vitex agnus-castus · chaste tree · Abraham's balm · monk's pepper

Not enough evidence to consider it unsafe, but one study shows ovarian hyper-stimulation.

May increase the risk of miscarriage
Sources:
  • “Herbal medicinal products during pregnancy: are they safe?”, E. Ernst
  • Vitex agnus-castus on Wikipedia

Dysphania

Also known as: Wormseed, Mexican tea, Epazote, Paico
Fine as occasional condiment May contain ascaridol (toxic compound) May induce uterine contractions Avoid ingesting oil, as it is neurotoxic.

Echinacea

Safety not established in some use cases, but it's generally discouraged for pregnant women and children under 12. Especial care must be taken to avoid lead-contaminated echinacea, and consumption should be avoided in the first trimester.

  • Samples found with lead contamination
  • May cause birth defects
  • May cause miscarriage
Sources:
  • “Herbal medicinal products during pregnancy: are they safe?”, E. Ernst
  • Echinacea on Wikipedia

Mentha pulegium

Also known as: Poleo, Pennyroyal
Essential oil is toxic to the liver Avoid internal consumption: abortifacient

Peumus boldus

Also known as: Boldo
May contain ascaridol (toxic compound) May contain boldine (may be dangerous to fetus)

Ricinus communis

Also known as: Ricino, Castor oil, palma Christi
Seeds are very poisonous Castor oil used as laxative may stimulate contractions

Rosmarinus officinalis

Also known as: Romero, Rosemary
Fine as occasional condiment Tea may induce contractions Topical use of essential oil should be avoided Ingestion of essential oil is dangerous

Ruta graveolens

Also known as: Rue, Ruda
May induce contractions Internal use may be toxic

Serenoa repens

Also known as: Palma sabal, Saw palmetto
Bark may induce contractions Unripe berries are toxic

Comfrey

Symphytum officinale

Contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which pass into breast milk.

Toxic to the baby's liver
Sources:
  • “The use of botanicals during pregnancy and lactation”, Tieraona Low Dog, MD

Borage

Borago officinalis

Contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which pass into breast milk.

Toxic to the baby's liver
Sources:
  • “The use of botanicals during pregnancy and lactation”, Tieraona Low Dog, MD

Safety not established

It's not clear whether these ingredients are safe, so it's best to avoid them during periods in which the fetus is more susceptible.

Ustilago maydis

Also known as: Corn smut, Cuitlacoche, Huitlacoche
Safety not established Avoid especially during the first trimester

Ginseng

Panax ginseng · Korean ginseng · Siberian ginseng · American ginseng · Panax quinquefolius

Cases exist where the intake of ginseng have a very negative impact on breastfed babies. Likely unsafe, but more studies are needed.

Safety not established

Piper methysticum

Also known as: Kava kava
Safety not established

Dangerous ⚠

None of these herbs should be used during pregnancy, regardless of application.

Artemisia spp.

Also known as: Estafiate, Istafiate, Ajenjo, Wormwood
Potentially neurotoxic

Caulophyllum thalictroides

Also known as: Cohosh azul, Blue cohosh, squaw root
May be toxic to the fetus May induce contractions

Datura

Also known as: Estramonio, toloache Jimsonweed, stramonium, moonflower
May be toxic Hallucinogenic

Dioscorea villosa

Also known as: Wild yam, Cabeza de negro
Uterine stimulant (may induce labor)

Feverfew

Tanacetum parthenium · tanaceto
May induce contractions May induce abortion

Guarana

Paullinia cupana
May cause birth defects May lead to low birthweight May cause premature birth

Licorice

Glycyrrhiza glabra · gan cao · glycyrrhizae extractum (crudum) · glycyrrhizae radix · kanzo · Licochalcone-A · liquiriti radix · shao-yao-gan-cao-tang · succens liquiritiae
Potentially abortifacient Neurotoxic to the baby Uterine stimulant (may induce labor)

Most problems are linked to high consumption levels (>250mg/week consumption of glycyrrhizin).

Sources: Drugs.com, University of Helsinki, study on Danish women
