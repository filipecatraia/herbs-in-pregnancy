Pregnancy and herbs

Herbs to avoid or restrict during pregnancy

Generally safe

These are considered generally safe at normal doses. Note that most of the herbs below may induce contractions, which can be painful. If normal consumption isn't causing contractions, usage should be relatively fine.

Risky (see notes)

The following are generally safe, but have uses that are risky. Please see the notes on each ingredient, to find out which applications are considered unsafe.

Dysphania

Also known as: Wormseed, Mexican tea, Epazote, Paico
Fine as occasional condiment May contain ascaridol (toxic compound) May induce uterine contractions Avoid ingesting oil, as it is neurotoxic.

Mentha pulegium

Also known as: Poleo, Pennyroyal
Essential oil is toxic to the liver Avoid internal consumption: abortifacient

Peumus boldus

Also known as: Boldo
May contain ascaridol (toxic compound) May contain boldine (may be dangerous to fetus)

Ricinus communis

Also known as: Ricino, Castor oil, palma Christi
Seeds are very poisonous Castor oil used as laxative may stimulate contractions

Rosmarinus officinalis

Also known as: Romero, Rosemary
Fine as occasional condiment Tea may induce contractions Topical use of essential oil should be avoided Ingestion of essential oil is dangerous

Ruta graveolens

Also known as: Rue, Ruda
May induce contractions Internal use may be toxic

Serenoa repens

Also known as: Palma sabal, Saw palmetto
Bark may induce contractions Unripe berries are toxic

Symphytum officinale

Also known as: Comfrey
Avoid prolonged use May be toxic to the liver

Safety not established

It's not clear whether these ingredients are safe, so it's best to avoid them during periods in which the fetus is more susceptible.

Ustilago maydis

Also known as: Corn smut, Cuitlacoche, Huitlacoche
Safety not established Avoid especially during the first trimester

Panax ginseng

Also known as: Ginseng coreano, Korean ginseng
Safety not established

Panax quinquefolius

Also known as: Ginseng americano, American ginseng
Safety not established

Piper methysticum

Also known as: Kava kava
Safety not established

Dangerous ⚠

None of these herbs should be used during pregnancy, regardless of application.

Artemisia spp.

Also known as: Estafiate, Istafiate, Ajenjo, Wormwood
Potentially neurotoxic

Caulophyllum thalictroides

Also known as: Cohosh azul, Blue cohosh, squaw root
May be toxic to the fetus May induce contractions

Datura

Also known as: Estramonio, toloache Jimsonweed, stramonium, moonflower
May be toxic Hallucinogenic

Dioscorea villosa

Also known as: Wild yam, Cabeza de negro
Uterine stimulant (may induce labor)
